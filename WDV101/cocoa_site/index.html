Some Facts About Cocoa:

Ever wonder what the difference is between cacao, cocoa, and chocolate?

  1. Cacao is the word used to refer to either the tree, Theobroma cacao, or the seed before roasting. The seeds form in pods which grow directly on the tree's trunk.
  2. Cocoa is the word used to refer to the seeds that have been roasted or the powder made from the roasted seeds. The powder can be mixed with warm milk and sweetners such as sugar to make modern hot cocoa.
  3. Chocolate is the food made from cocoa, and may contain any combination of cocoa solids, cocoa butter, fat, and sugar. Sweet chocolate contains all of these ingredients. Baking chocolate, or bitter chocolate, is cocoa solids and cocoa butter only. To create milk chocolate, powdered or condensed milk is added. White chocolate does not contain any cocoa solids.
